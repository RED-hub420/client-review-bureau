Review handling

Review handling and publication standards

Reference standards for filing, notice, response handling, publication, and correction requests.

Submission standardsPublication criteria

1. Directory purpose

CRB publishes screened directory records tied to marketplace transactions and attempted transactions. Public records are limited summaries and do not include raw uploads, internal notes, or unrestricted personal information.

2. What may be filed

Accepted matters may involve delivery issues, appointment losses, chargebacks, repeated nonpayment, property damage, deposit reversals, unreturned rented items, and similar marketplace issues supported by records.

3. What will not be handled

  • Employment or workplace disputes
  • Medical, family, or domestic matters
  • Unsupported criminal accusations
  • Threats, retaliation, or abusive language
  • Complaints unrelated to a marketplace relationship

4. Filing standards

Filings must identify the reported party with enough detail for screening, include a date and transaction reference, and contain a concise factual summary. CRB may request supporting records before a file moves into notice or publication review.

5. Notice and response handling

After a filing is accepted, notice may be sent to the reported party and a response window may open. Review handling generally begins within two business days, responses are commonly requested within 14 days, and many matters are updated, limited, or closed within about 30 days.

6. Publication criteria

A public file may show pending review, answered, dispute open, unanswered, limited summary, or closed status depending on the record history and supporting information on file.

7. Corrections and disputes

Correction requests, response statements, and disputes remain attached to the same record. CRB may reduce public detail, update status, or close a matter based on information received during review handling.

8. What publication does not mean

Publication does not constitute a legal finding, regulatory action, criminal determination, or consumer credit decision.
