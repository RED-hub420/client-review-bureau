Policy and process

Member Reporting Standards

The network only works if reporting stays factual, limited, and tied to a real transaction.

Version 1.4 Current policy

1. Who may submit reports

Only verified member businesses may submit incident records. Each member account must be tied to a real operating business, a named contact, and an approved use category. Anonymous submissions are not accepted.

2. What a report must include

Reports must identify the transaction date, a work order or invoice reference, an incident category, and a clear description of what occurred. When available, members should include supporting documents, delivery records, signed approvals, route logs, or processor notices.

3. What is prohibited

  • Insults, threats, or retaliatory language
  • Unsupported accusations of criminal conduct
  • Irrelevant personal information or doxxing
  • Protected medical, financial, or identity details not needed for the record
  • Speculation about motive, character, or lifestyle

4. Visibility rules

Reports may be saved as internal-only, restricted member visible, or elevated to broader network visibility after review. Visibility depends on documentation level, moderation outcome, and dispute status.

5. Disputes and corrections

If a challenged record enters dispute review, visibility may be reduced until the member provides additional documentation or the record is corrected, withdrawn, or locked. All status changes remain attached to the original incident trail.

Moderator checklist

Internal
  • Confirm the business is active and verified.
  • Check category fit and visibility level.
  • Review attached evidence and redactions.
  • Reduce visibility if the dispute threshold is met.
  • Lock record after final determination.

Rule objective

Reference

The goal is not public shaming. The goal is a narrow, auditable system for documented transaction risk, operational loss, and repeat issue tracking inside a verified business network.
