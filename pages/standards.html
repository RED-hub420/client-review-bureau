Review process

How reviews are accepted, handled, and published

These standards are what make the concept feel like a bureau and not a revenge wall.

Acceptance guidelines Publication standards

1. Review acceptance guidelines

A filing must come from a verified business, identify the other party with enough detail to review the matter, and relate to a marketplace issue involving a product, service, appointment, release, payment, or delivery.

2. Reviews must stay tied to a real transaction

Each file must include an event date, a reference number or work order, and a concise statement of what occurred. Bureau staff can request invoices, delivery confirmations, processor notices, call logs, or signed documents.

3. What will not be handled or published

  • Abusive language, threats, or retaliation
  • Unsupported allegations of criminal conduct
  • Workplace or employment disputes
  • Medical, identity, or unrelated private financial information
  • Complaints that do not involve a marketplace relationship

4. Notice and response process

After a filing is accepted for handling, the bureau can notify the reported party and request a response. This demo uses a bureau-style flow: notice within about two business days, a response window of about two weeks, and a typical handling cycle that aims to close within roughly 30 days when both sides participate.

5. Publication and status rules

Some files are never published. Others are published only in summary form. A file can show as answered, unresolved, unanswered, dispute open, or closed depending on the history attached to that record.

6. Membership standards

Member businesses must provide real operating information, use the bureau only for allowed filing types, and agree not to submit speculative, retaliatory, or unsupported reviews. Membership can be limited or removed for misuse.

Typical handling timeline

Working model
  • Within 2 business days Accepted filings move into bureau notice or document review.
  • Within 14 days The other party is typically asked to respond.
  • Within 30 days Many files are closed, updated, or published in final status.
