File a review

Verified businesses may submit a documented review tied to a transaction, attempted transaction, delivery issue, chargeback, nonpayment, or related marketplace matter.

For businesses

Member businesses may submit review records, attach supporting documentation, and respond to matters connected to their public record.

Review Process

1

Review submitted

A member business submits a review record and supporting documentation.

2

Initial screening completed

The filing is reviewed for completeness, fit, and publication standards.

3

Notice sent to listed party

When the matter is accepted, notice is issued and the response window begins.

4

Response period

Responses, dispute notes, and supporting items are added to the same file.

5

File updated and published

The public file is updated with current status, response history, and closure notes when applicable.

What appears in a public file

Directory use
  • Published summary of the review matter
  • Current bureau status and last activity date
  • Response history when one has been received
  • 3-year file totals and basic issue categories

What does not appear

Protected
  • Payment account details, identity numbers, or raw processor notices
  • Unscreened uploads, private notes, or internal handling comments
  • Medical details, unrelated personal information, or doxxing-level data
  • Any statement presented as a court finding or legal determination
