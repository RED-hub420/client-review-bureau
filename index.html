How reviews are handled

Modeled on a bureau process, not a social feed.

Reviews move through intake, screening, notice, response, and status updates. The goal is a usable marketplace record, not an open rant board.

1

Review is submitted

A verified business files a review tied to a real transaction and selects a file type, date, amount, and summary.

2

Bureau screens the submission

Staff checks the file for fit, abusive language, unsupported claims, duplicate reporting, and publication readiness.

3

Reported party is notified

When a file is accepted for handling, the reported party is contacted and given a response window before final publication status is set.

4

Status is updated

Files may be published as answered, unresolved, unanswered, or closed, depending on the documentation and response activity.

5

History stays attached to the same file

Updates, disputes, corrections, and closure notes remain attached to the same record so later viewers can see the file trail.

Publication standards

Summary
  • The review must relate to a marketplace transaction or attempted transaction.
  • The filing business must provide enough information to identify the client or business involved.
  • Threats, insults, and unsupported criminal accusations are not published.
  • Publication status can change if a dispute is opened or supporting material is missing.
