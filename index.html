Built for operating businesses

The platform is meant for rental companies, contractors, suppliers, service shops, and other teams that deal with real transaction loss, repeat nonpayment, or high-friction customer disputes.

Structured records, not open-ended rants

Every report is tied to a date, reference number, incident type, visibility level, and supporting detail. Freeform accusations are not the product.

Visibility is controlled

Member businesses can maintain internal-only records, share restricted flags with the network, and respond to disputes inside the same audit trail.

Common report categories

Focused on operational loss and documented disputes

Categories stay narrow on purpose. The system is designed for transaction issues that can be documented, reviewed, and tied back to an actual business event.

No-show / appointment loss

Scheduled service visit, delivery, install, inspection, or rental pickup missed with no notice.

Payment reversal / chargeback

Completed order or service later reversed through card dispute, processor claim, or bank pullback.

Fraudulent return claim

Item used, swapped, damaged, or materially altered and then returned with a false condition claim.

Repeated nonpayment

Customer took delivery, received service, or signed approval but failed to pay on multiple instances.

Damage during rental / possession

Equipment, trailer, tool, or supplied materials returned damaged outside expected wear standards.

Abusive or unsafe conduct

Threats, harassment, unsafe jobsite behavior, or employee-directed abuse during a documented transaction.

How it works

A practical record flow businesses can actually use

1

Member account is verified

Company identity, primary contact, website, and operating category are reviewed before network access is approved.

2

Incident is tied to a transaction

Reports are created against an invoice, appointment, work order, contract, or reference ID with a date and event type.

3

Visibility and evidence are set

Businesses choose internal-only, restricted member view, or full-network report status based on documentation level.

4

Disputes are handled inside the same record

Member review, record locking, notes, and status changes stay attached to the original incident for auditability.

Standards

The product only works if the reporting rules are tight.

Reports must stay factual, specific, and connected to a real transaction. Unsupported accusations, personal attacks, irrelevant personal data, and speculative claims are prohibited.

Read member reporting standards

Submission minimums

  • Verified business account
  • Transaction date and reference number
  • Incident category and visibility level
  • Loss amount or operational impact
  • Supporting notes or documentation
  • Agreement to reporting standards
