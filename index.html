What this is

A private member record network for documented customer incidents, operational loss events, and dispute handling tied to actual transactions.

What this is not

It is not a public complaint board, consumer review wall, or open posting system. Visibility is controlled and unsupported accusations are removed.

Who uses it

Rental companies, contractors, suppliers, towing operators, service shops, and other businesses that deal with payment loss, misuse, no-shows, and release disputes.

Common file types

Records stay narrow on purpose.

Member files are limited to issues that can be documented, reviewed, and tied back to a real order, work order, job, rental, service call, pickup, or delivery.

No-show / labor loss

Scheduled service, install, delivery, inspection, or pickup missed with no workable notice.

Payment reversal / chargeback

Completed order or service later reversed through bank dispute, processor claim, or pullback.

Fraudulent return claim

Returned item swapped, used, damaged, incomplete, or falsely described during the claim.

Repeated nonpayment

Customer received goods or service and has more than one unpaid balance in the lookback period.

Damage during possession

Equipment, tools, materials, or rented units returned with damage outside expected wear.

Unsafe or abusive conduct

Threats, harassment, unsafe site behavior, or employee-directed abuse during a documented job.

File handling

How a member file moves through the system

1

Business account is verified

Company identity, primary contact, site, and operating category are reviewed before access is granted.

2

Record is opened against a transaction

Members use an invoice, work order, contract, appointment, route ID, or delivery reference to anchor the file.

3

Visibility is set by documentation level

Records can stay internal, move to restricted member view, or remain limited until further proof is uploaded.

4

Disputes and corrections stay in the same file

Notes, evidence requests, visibility changes, and lock decisions stay attached to the original record.

Standards first

Required

The system only works if records stay factual, limited, and tied to supportable events. Unsupported claims, doxxing, emotional complaints, and irrelevant personal data are outside the rules.

Open reporting standards

Business intake
